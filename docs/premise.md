# Premise

Vesprium is a world built around **biohacking**: sentient beings deliberately reshape themselves by weaving **technology**, **other living organisms**, and **subtle magic** into their bodies and minds. Improvement is not an abstract ideal—it is a practiced art, a social force, and often a moral battlefield.

## What makes it unique

**Integration, not replacement.** Enhancement here is rarely “swap a limb for a machine and call it done.” Success usually means **symbiosis**: tissues that learn, interfaces that grow in, grafts that negotiate with the host’s immune and nervous systems. Technology and biology are expected to **co-evolve**, not merely coexist.

**Magic is quiet, precise, and infrastructural.** Magic in Vesprium is not fireworks and shouting. It is **subtle**—a layer of possibility that makes certain unions stable, certain signals intelligible across species, or certain boundaries permeable without violence. It is the reason extreme integrations can work at all: the glue, the catalyst, the grammar that lets flesh, metal, and foreign life understand one another well enough to become **one system**.

**The impossible becomes craft.** Real-world limits on graft rejection, neural bandwidth, metabolic load, and ecological compatibility are **bent**, not ignored. The world still cares about consequence—cost, risk, identity drift, dependency—but the design space for “what a person can become” is vastly wider than our own. Stories and systems can explore **how far** to push, **who** gets to push, and **what is lost** when the body stops being a fixed address.

## Core tensions (seeds for conflict and software)

- **Autonomy vs. dependence** — Who owns an enhancement when it is alive, networked, or magically entangled?
- **Purity vs. pragmatism** — Cultural and political reactions to “how much” integration is acceptable.
- **Consent across species** — Organisms used as components are not props; ethical and narrative weight applies.
- **Identity** — When the self is edited, shared, or distributed across symbionts, what remains “you”?

## Relationship to this repository

This monorepo will eventually host multiple programs—tools, games, simulations, or narratives—that assume this premise. Documents here should stay **setting-first**: stable ideas about the world; implementation details belong beside the code that uses them.
